# NYC Food and Transit Notes

## Food Areas

Travelers interested in casual food diversity should consider the East Village, Koreatown, and parts of Queens for future expansions, while Manhattan-focused trips can still find strong food corridors in the West Village and Lower East Side.

## Transit Tips

The subway keeps New York flexible, but fast itineraries often underestimate transfer fatigue. A better plan clusters neighborhoods and limits unnecessary borough-hopping, especially for shorter trips.

## Budget Notes

Free park time, walking-heavy neighborhoods, and a mix of one marquee attraction plus lower-cost food stops help keep NYC within a mid-range budget.
