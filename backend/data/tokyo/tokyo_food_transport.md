# Tokyo Food and Transport Notes

## Food Districts

Shinjuku and Shibuya are useful for broad food variety, but travelers who want a more market-oriented or street-snack feel should consider Tsukiji Outer Market, Ameya-Yokocho near Ueno, and smaller izakaya alleys in neighborhoods like Omoide Yokocho.

## Transport Tips

Tokyo is easiest to navigate when each day stays geographically focused. The JR Yamanote Line connects many popular districts, while the subway fills in shorter urban hops. Travelers with slower pace preferences should avoid stacking too many station changes in a single afternoon.

## Budget Notes

Convenience store breakfasts, lunch sets, and neighborhood noodle shops can keep a Tokyo trip in the mid-range or budget category without sacrificing quality.
